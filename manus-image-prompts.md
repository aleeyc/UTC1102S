## CONTEXT

This is an interactive narrative game set in Nairobi, Kenya, spanning 2026 to 2045. The story follows a scientist who accidentally time-travels through a mysterious ring and discovers that their own neural chip prototype has been developed into a mass-deployed product by a corporation called Qoogle. The Q-chip -- a small rectangular device worn on the temple -- has become the backbone of civic life across Africa, with profound consequences for who gets saved during disasters.

The story is told through 17 in-game scene illustrations and 2 in-game document props (a newspaper and a corporate flyer). All 19 images must feel like they belong in the same visual universe.

**Please read the full game script (provided separately) before generating any images.** Visual continuity across all 19 images is critical.

### THE Q-CHIP (MOST CRITICAL -- READ CAREFULLY)

The Q-chip is a small, thin, RECTANGULAR device. It is approximately 12mm wide by 8mm tall -- roughly the size of a microSD card or a small postage stamp. It has slightly rounded corners and is made of brushed silver metal with a faint blue-tinged sheen.

**Placement:** It adheres flat against the RIGHT TEMPLE -- the area of skin between the outer corner of the eye and the ear, on the side of the forehead. It sits flush against the skin with no visible gap, no strap, no adhesive edges. It looks embedded, like it was placed there and simply stayed.

**Shape rules -- STRICTLY ENFORCED:**
- The chip is RECTANGULAR. Not circular. Not a disc. Not round. Not oval. Not a dot.
- It is flat and thin -- no thicker than a credit card.
- It has the proportions of a small rectangle, wider than it is tall.
- A micro-etched Q logo is on its surface, only visible at very close range.
- When active or catching light, the edges emit the faintest blue glow -- similar to a tiny LED indicator.

**What it is NOT:**
- It is NOT a CD, disc, or circle of any size.
- It is NOT a large device. It is small and sleek.
- It is NOT a headband, visor, or glasses.
- It is NOT a glowing circle or dot.

**Consistency:** Every person wearing a Q-chip wears it in the exact same position (right temple), at the exact same size, in the exact same rectangular shape. Whether the wearer is a child, an adult, a vendor, or a government official, the chip looks identical. When seen at a distance on pedestrians, the chip reads as a small rectangular silver-blue glint at the temple -- subtle but noticeable once you know to look.

**Who wears it:** In 2040 and 2045 scenes, virtually every person in Nairobi has one. The uniformity should be subtly unsettling -- row after row of people, each with the same small silver rectangle in the same spot.

**Who does NOT wear it:** Zara (in both her scenes) does not have a chip. The protagonist in 2026 scenes does not have one. These absences should be noticeable by contrast.

The androids in this world are designed to pass as human at first glance. They are NOT obviously robotic -- no visible metal plating, no glowing eyes, no seams, no panels, no exposed wiring, no chrome skin.

They look like attractive, well-groomed humans with excellent posture. The uncanny tells are subtle:
- Their skin has a very slight waxy smoothness -- like high-end silicone, almost indistinguishable from real skin except in how evenly it reflects light.
- Their facial symmetry is a fraction too perfect.
- Their expressions are warm but arrive a half-beat too precisely -- as if each smile or head-tilt was computed rather than felt.
- Their eyes are the primary uncanny marker: a faint golden-amber iris colour that no natural human has, and a gaze that holds just slightly too steadily without micro-movements.

**Q-SA4** (Qoogle Store associate): Wears a simple white store uniform. Badge on lapel. Gender-neutral to slightly masculine presentation. Calm, helpful demeanour.

**ARIA-3** (civil information officer): Wears a pale blue uniform -- Nairobi Public Information Service -- pressed without a crease. Feminine presentation. Professional, measured, institutional.

Both should look human enough that you would need a second look to realize something is off.

- **Aspect ratio:** Widescreen 16:9 (1.9:1 acceptable). 1440x756px minimum resolution.
- **No text, UI, logos, or watermarks baked into images.** The game engine overlays its own text and interface. Images must be clean.
- **Faces:** No faces shown in sharp, full-frontal detail. Faces should be in partial shadow, turned to three-quarter angle, slightly soft focus, or at enough distance that features are suggested rather than photographic. This applies to all human and android characters.
- **Style:** Digital illustration, cinematic composition. Semi-realistic painterly style with soft edges. Muted, atmospheric colour palette. Subtle film grain texture across all images. Inspired by the visual tone of Blade Runner 2049 crossed with contemporary East African architecture and landscape.
- **Consistency:** All 19 images must feel like frames from the same film. Colour grading, grain, style, and level of detail should be uniform.
- **Colour evolution across timeline:**
  - 2026 scenes: Warm amber, sepia, golden afternoon tones.
  - March 2040: Warm African sunlight mixed with cool technological blue.
  - November 2040: Desaturated, dusty, harder light. Muted palette with grey and brown.
  - 2045: Clean, cold, corporate. Deep blues and chrome.
  - Epilogue (back to 2026): Warm amber again, but darker -- underground, single light source.
